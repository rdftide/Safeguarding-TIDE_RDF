⚠️ Project Safeguarding Risk Profile

This tool supports safeguarding implementation for the Transforming Gender Inequality and Ensuring Empowerment for Climate-induced Displaced People in Urban Slum project. It covers 4 slums in Barishal City Corporation (Wards 6, 9, 11), with 4,778 direct participants (80% women/girls).

● High Risk
Child Marriage & GBV
Project area has high child marriage rates. Activities involving adolescent girls require heightened oversight and safeguarding protocols.
● High Risk
PSEA in Slum Settings
Dense informal settlements and power imbalances between staff/volunteers and displaced beneficiaries create elevated PSEA risk.
● High Risk
MHM & Dignity Risks
Poor sanitation infrastructure and lack of private toilets increase dignity and safety risks for women and girls, especially at night.
● Medium Risk
Childcare Centre Risks
Establishment of childcare centres requires child protection screening of all staff and robust supervision protocols.
● Medium Risk
Data Privacy
Collecting sensitive data on 3,150 climate migrants requires data protection measures and informed consent processes.
● Lower Risk
Vocational Training
Training activities for women/girls should be monitored for undue influence. Safe transport arrangements needed.
● Lower Risk
Waste Management
Community engagement activities should ensure inclusive participation without excluding vulnerable groups.

📜 Core Safeguarding Principles for This Project
1
Do No Harm

All project activities — from WASH infrastructure to vocational training — must be designed and monitored to avoid causing harm to participants, particularly women, girls, and children in informal settlements who may be in vulnerable situations due to climate displacement.

2
Survivor-Centred Approach

The wishes, safety, and wellbeing of survivors of abuse or exploitation come first. Confidentiality is paramount. Survivors must give informed consent before any action is taken on their behalf. Staff must never pressure survivors to report to authorities.

3
Child Protection Priority

Given the high rates of child marriage in the project area and the operation of childcare centres, child safeguarding is a top priority. No child shall be subjected to abuse, exploitation, or unsafe conditions within any project activity or facility.

4
Zero Tolerance for SEA

Sexual exploitation and abuse by any project staff, volunteer, or associated personnel is absolutely prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action and referral to relevant authorities. Power imbalances between staff and climate-displaced beneficiaries must be actively managed.

5
Inclusion & Non-Discrimination

Safeguarding protections apply equally to all beneficiaries regardless of gender, age, disability, religion, or ethnicity. The 80% women/girls target group requires gender-responsive safeguarding measures including safe spaces, female staff, and culturally appropriate reporting mechanisms.

6
Accountability & Transparency

RDF is accountable to the communities it serves, to CBF/CCW, and to all project participants. Safeguarding performance must be reported transparently in all donor reports. Community feedback mechanisms must be functional and trusted.

7
Confidentiality

Information about safeguarding concerns and incidents is strictly confidential and shared only on a need-to-know basis. Breaching confidentiality (except where legally required to protect a child or vulnerable adult) is itself a safeguarding violation.
